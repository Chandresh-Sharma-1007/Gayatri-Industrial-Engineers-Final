Complete Guide for Textile Manufacturers | Gayatri Industrial Engineers In the modern textile industry, fabric finishing is what defines quality. What is Decatising in Textile Processing?

Decatisingis a steam-based fabric finishing process used to improve the dimensional stability, surface finish, and handle of fabrics.

It is widely used in:

  • Wool fabric finishing
  • Wool blend fabric processing
  • Suiting fabric manufacturing
    • This process ensures that fabrics maintain their shape, texture, and appearance even after washing, pressing, or long-term use.

    What is a Decatising Machine?

    A Decatising Machineis atextile finishing equipmentdesigned to treat fabrics using controlled steam, heat, and pressure.

    It plays a key role in:

  • Reducing fabric shrinkage
  • Enhancing fabric luster and smoothness
  • Improving fabric hand feel
  • Setting the fabric structure permanently
    • At Gayatri Industrial Engineers, our machines are built to deliver precision finishing, energy efficiency, and long-term reliability.

    How Does a strDecatising Machine Work?

    The decatising process follows a structured workflow to ensure uniform results:

  • Fabric Winding on Perforated Roller:- Fabric is wound along with a wrapper cloth to ensure even steam penetration.
  • Steam Application:- High-pressure steam passes through the fabric layers, conditioning the fibers.
  • Heat & Pressure Setting:- Controlled temperature and pressure help set the fabric structure.
  • Cooling & Stabilization:-Cooling locks in the finish and ensures dimensional stability.
  • Fabric Unwinding;- The finished fabric is ready for further processing or dispatch.

    What are the Different Types of Singeing in Textile?

    Singeing can be performed using various methods, each suited to different fabric types and production requirements. Understanding these types helps in selecting the appropriate machine.

    1. Gas Singeing

      2. This is the most common method, where fabric passes over a gas flame. It is suitable for cotton, polyester, and blended fabrics. Gas singeing machines are widely used due to their efficiency and relatively low operating costs.

    2. Plate Singeing

      3. In this method, fabric contacts a heated metal plate that burns off surface fibers. Plate singeing is less common but useful for delicate fabrics that may be damaged by open flames.

    3. Biogas Singeing

      4. An eco-friendly alternative, biogas singeing uses biogas as fuel instead of conventional gas. This method reduces environmental impact and can be cost-effective in regions with biogas availability.

    4. Infrared Singeing

      5. Infrared singeing uses infrared radiation to remove fibers without direct flame contact. It is suitable for sensitive fabrics and offers precise control but is generally more expensive.

    5. Singeing with Steam

      6. Some machines combine singeing with steam treatment to improve fabric smoothness and reduce static. This hybrid approach enhances fabric quality but requires more complex machinery.

    Decatising Machines: Enhance Fabric Quality in Textile Finishing Equipment

    6 min read

    In the textile industry, fabric finishing plays a crucial role in determining the final quality and appearance of textiles. Among the various finishing processes, decatising stands out as a vital step to improve fabric texture, stability, and sheen. This process involves the use of specialized machines designed to treat fabrics under controlled heat and pressure, ensuring they maintain their shape and look after washing or use.


    Decatising machines are essential in fabric finishing equipment setups, especially for wool, polyester, and blended fabrics. They help remove creases, set the fabric’s dimensions, and enhance the overall hand feel. This article explores how decatising machines contribute to fabric quality, the types of machines available, and practical tips for optimizing their use in textile finishing.

    Understanding Fabric Finishing Equipment and Its Importance

    Fabric finishing equipment encompasses a range of machines and tools used to enhance the physical and aesthetic properties of textiles after weaving or knitting. These processes include washing, drying, calendaring, and decatising, among others. The goal is to improve fabric durability, appearance, and performance.
    Fabric finishing equipment is critical because untreated fabrics often have residual stresses from weaving or knitting, which can cause shrinkage, distortion, or uneven texture. Proper finishing ensures that fabrics meet quality standards and customer expectations.

    Key Benefits of Fabric Finishing Equipment

  • Improved fabric stability: Reduces shrinkage and distortion during use.
  • Enhanced texture and softness: Makes fabrics more comfortable to wear.
  • Better appearance: Adds sheen and smoothness to the fabric surface.
  • Increased durability: Strengthens fabric fibers for longer life.
  • Consistent dimensions: Ensures uniform size and shape for garment production.

    • Using the right fabric finishing equipment, including decatising machines, is essential for textile manufacturers aiming to deliver premium quality products.

    Types of Decatising Machines and Their Functions

    Decatising machines come in various designs, each suited for specific fabric types and finishing requirements. Understanding these types helps manufacturers select the best equipment for their production lines.

    1. Roller Decatising Machines

    These machines use heated rollers to press and smooth the fabric. The fabric passes between the rollers under controlled temperature and pressure, which sets the fabric’s dimensions and removes wrinkles.

  • Suitable for wool and wool-blend fabrics.
  • Provides a smooth, glossy finish.
  • Ideal for continuous processing.

    • Steam Decatising Machines

    Steam decatising involves exposing fabric to steam in a controlled environment, often inside a chamber or drum. The steam relaxes the fibers, allowing them to be reshaped and set.

  • Best for delicate fabrics like silk and fine wool.
  • Helps maintain fabric softness.
  • Can be batch or continuous process.

    • 3. Vacuum Decatising Machines

    These machines combine steam treatment with vacuum pressure to enhance fabric penetration and drying. The vacuum helps remove excess moisture quickly, reducing processing time

  • Suitable for high-volume production.
  • Ensures uniform fabric treatment.
  • Reduces energy consumption.

    The Role of Singeing in Textile Processing

    Singeing plays a pivotal role in textile preparation. This process involves passing the fabric through a flame to burn off any lint, fuzz, or loose fibers that may be present on the surface.
